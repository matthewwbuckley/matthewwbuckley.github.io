System Fonts

  • typography
  • design
  • html

San Francisco on Mac. Segoe on Windows. Both designed by people smarter than me, optimized for screens, free in every way.

Zero kilobytes. Sometimes the best choice is no choice.

I used to spend hours picking typefaces. Browsing Google Fonts like it was a museum. Loading three weights of Inter plus a display font for headings. The CSS alone was 200kb before a single word rendered.

Then I discovered the system font stack. One line of CSS, no network requests, instant rendering. The font is already there, cached in the operating system itself.

The irony is these system fonts are better than most of what I was choosing. Apple employs typographers who’ve spent years refining San Francisco for screens. Microsoft did the same with Segoe. These aren’t compromises—they’re the work of experts who had resources I’ll never have.

There’s a certain humility in accepting defaults. Not every decision needs to be a statement. Sometimes the background should just be a background, quietly doing its job while the content takes center stage.
